About

Jessica Ahn, born in 2003 in Chicago, Illinois, is an artist residing in Columbia, Missouri, completing a BA in Art at the School of Visual Studies at the University of Missouri. Before going to university, she resided in St. Louis, which is where she grew inspired to become an artist. Primarily working with painting and ceramics, she continues to expand her horizons through other mediums such as yarn and chalk.

While attending school, she has taken part in a community mural project, titled Stuff Your Eyes With Wonder, which is located in the stairwell of the Fine Arts Building at the University of Missouri. She has also taken a year to study abroad in Seoul, South Korea, exploring both her own cultural roots, as well as viewing art through a Korean lens. Her expected graduation is December 2025.

Artist Statement

Using various mediums, I use portraiture and scenes from everyday life to illustrate memories from my childhood. From happiness to loneliness, I try to capture the bittersweet emotions associated with growing up. To me, portraiture is almost like a love letter, in the sense that you try to capture whoever you are painting as the way that you see them through your eyes, and yours alone. Through painting people, you can take a glimpse into their personal lives, quirks, and what makes them unique.

My work centers around my childhood, and how it shaped me into who I am today. Through mediums like chalk and pen, I wanted to capture the impermanence of our youth, and how our memories tend to distort our perceptions of ourselves as we age. All of my work is inspired by the shared emotions between the people in my life, from joy all the way to grief. Grief, while universal, is reflected so differently from person to person, that I wanted to share my own story on what it means to lose someone dear to you. The sadness may never disappear, but it does not erase the joy left behind.
